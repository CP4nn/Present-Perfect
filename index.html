Present Perfect

The Present Perfect is used to talk about actions or experiences that connect the past to the present. ItÂ´s not about when something happened - but rather it has happened and still matters now.

  • Estructure: Subject + have/has + past participle
    • Example (Positive): I have eaten sushi before.
    Example (Negative): He has not called me.
  • Question Estructure: Have/has + subject + past participle + question mark(?)
    • Example: Have You seen that movie?

Why can use the Present Perfect to:

  • Talk about life experiences:

    I have visited Paris.

  • Describe changes over time:

    She has grown a lot this year.

  • Mention recent actions:

    They have just arrived.

  • Refer to unfinished time periods:

    We have studied all morning.


Adverbs of Time
Ever Asking about life experiences.
Never Saying something has not happened.
Just Describes a very recent action.
Already Action happened earlier than expected.
Yet Used in questions and negatives (still pending).
For Duration of time (how long).
Since Starting point in time.
